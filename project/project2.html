Myocardial Infarction Detection System Using Arduino

"Arduino project to detect a person suffering from Myocardial Infraction by using Pathological Q Waves and ST Segment Elevation as features"

Github Repository

What background is the underlying work on this project?

According to the World Health Organization (WHO) deaths caused by Heart disease in the world reach 17.9 million annually, or about 32% of deaths worldwide. while in Indonesia according to the Ministry of Health in 2013 heart disease was the First cause of death in Indonesia with a rate of 37% compared to other diseases.

What is Myocardial Infarction ?

Myocardial infraction occurs when one or more areas of the Heart do not get enough oxygen supply due to blocked blood flow to the heart. Blocked blood flow to the heart is caused by plaque buildup in the arteries (atherosclerosis). When plaque ruptures or disintegrates it will form a blood clot, this blood clot is the cause of Myocardial Infraction.

How to Diagnose if Someone Suffers Myocardial Infarction ?

Myocardial infraction itself has some symptoms such as pain and tightness in the chest. Patients who have these symptoms usually do a test to determine whether they have Myocardial Infraction or not. There are 2 ways that can be done to detect someone suffering from Myocardial Infarction, namely by doing an Electrocardiogram (ECG) test or by doing a blood test.

What Method is Used to Detect Myocardial Infarction ?

Blood tests are tested because some heart proteins will slowly leak into the blood due to Myocardial Infarction. Blood tests are done 2 times for 6-24 hours. Because it takes a long time blood test is not a good solution in detecting Myocardial Infraction so the ECG test is selected.

How Electrocardiogram Test Works ?

Electrocardiogram is a simple test that can be used to check your heart's rhythm and electrical activity. Sensors attached to the skin are used to detect the electrical signals produced by your heart each time it beats.

Detect the Myocardial Infarction based on ECG Signal

People with Myocardial Infraction can be detected by performing electrocardiography tests and reading the results of the signal. Because in Myocardial Infarction sufferers have some differences with normal signal results.

What is Pathological Q Waves ?

Pathological Q Waves is one of the clues that can be seen in the ECG signal that indicates that a person is suffering from or has suffered from Myocardial Infarction. Pathological Q Waves indicate the absence of electrical activity on the heart.

Q Waves called Pathological Q Waves :
A. Has a depth of more than 25% compared to QRS Complex
B. Has a width of more than 40 ms or about 1 mm
C. Has a depth of more than 2mm
D. Pathological Q Waves can be seen in Lead V1-V3

What is ST Segment Elevation ?

The ST Segment represents the interval between depolarization and ventricular repolarization. One of the abnormalities that can occur in the ST Segment is an increase (Elevation) which is one of the signs of Myocardial Infarction . ST Segment elevation occurs at the J point which has a value of ≥ 0.2 mV for men and ≥ 0.15 mV for women which can be seen in the lead V2-V3.

Why use Pathological Q Waves and ST Segment Elevation as features?

ST Segment elevation is used as a feature because in previous studies it used ST Segment Elevation as a feature and got an accuracy of 91.89% (Anirudha V Bhardwaj, et al. 2018). Pathological Q Waves are used because they are a feature that is often encountered by people with myocardial infraction. In other studies discussing these two features are indeed present in patients with Myocardial Infarction (MS Guillem, et al. 2004)

Classify Using Support Vector Machine

Support Vector Machine (SVM) is a linear model for classification and regression problems. It can solve linear and non-linear problems and work well for many practical problems. Both conditions are then used as a feature to be used to classify myocardial infraction sufferers and normal using support vector machine

How to get electrical activity on the heart ?

Sensor Module AD8232 is a tool for measuring electrical activity in the heart with a Single Lead . When conducting electrical activity readings on the heart using electrodes. electrodes must be placed on a certain part of the surface of the skin. Electrode placement using Einthoven's Triangle theory. Einthoven's Triangle is an imaginary triangular formation used in the placement of electrodes when reading electrical activity in the heart.

The system works by using the AD8232 sensor to get the ECG signal then processing it on the arduino and displaying the result on LCD16x2
